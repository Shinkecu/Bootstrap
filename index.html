Gocha Russian drift series champion

About

Georgy Tumasovich Chivchyan is a Russian racing driver-drifter, Champion of Siberia 2012, three-time Champion of Russia, driver of the Japanese D1GP Championship 2013, D1GP participant, champion of the Russian GP Drift Series 2017, 2018, 2019. 2018 and 2019 - winner of the FIA ​​Intercontinental Drifting Cup.

Since 2008, when the level of Krasnoyarsk drifters increased, drift competitions in Krasnoyarsk took on a new look and began to be held on the new Red Ring race track. From the very first stages and until now, in any competition, Georgy is considered one of the main favorites. Since then, “Gocha” has been considered the strongest drifter in Russia, which he has proven throughout his career not only in Siberia, but also in the western part of Russia. In 2013, he made his first trip to the west of Russia to participate in the RDS West competition, but Georgy managed to take part in only two stages, unfortunately, due to slightly different styles, he was not able to take prizes; the fact that he came also made it difficult to adapt one from his region, after which the rest began to join in - Arkady Tsaregradtsev, Dmitry Ermokhin, Evgeny Losev, Damir Idiyatulin. But already in 2015, Gocha began to show everyone that he was the strongest drifter in Russia and the more he drove, the higher his place in the overall ranking became. So in 2017, for the first time taking part along with Western pilots in all stages of the series, he won by a huge margin.[10] In 2018, Georgy successfully defended the title of champion despite the appearance in the championship of foreign pilots invited to the stages and the world famous pilot Daigo Saito constantly participating in the championship[11]. Chivchyan confirmed his skills in 2019, becoming the only 3-time RDS champion
